Empire State Building


Although it’s no longer the tallest building in New York City, this world-famous landmark remains a Manhattan icon for good reason. On the 86th floor is the highest open-air observatory in NYC, offering a 360-degree views of the Hudson and East Rivers, the Brooklyn Bridge, the Statue of Liberty and much more.

High Line


Escape to an Oasis in the middle of the hustle and bustle NYC. High Line is a former rail line transformed into an urban walking trail above the city streets. Hight Line has been planted with a variety of plants and trees, many of which are native species, and lined with glass railings in most areas, giving it a natural feel, while still offering outstanding views of the city.

Eiffel Tower


Built by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, in celebration of the 100th year anniversary of the French Revolution. The Tower represents the magic of light. Its lighting, its sparkling lights, and its beacon shine and inspire dreams every evening. As France’s symbol in the world, and the showcase of Paris, today it welcomes almost 7 million visitors a year

Arc de Triomphe


The Arc de Triomphe Paris, the most monumental of all triumphal arches, was built between 1806 and 1836. The monument is considered the linchpin of the historic axis (L'Axe historique) and the triumphal arch is actully older than the Eiffel Tower.
